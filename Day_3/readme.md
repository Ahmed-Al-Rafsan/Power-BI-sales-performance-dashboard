# Power BI Project 3 – Profit \& Loss Insights



**Project Overview**

This project focuses on analyzing \*\*profit and loss performance\*\* using Power BI.  

The goal is to help decision-makers quickly identify \*\*profitable areas, loss-making sub-categories, and overall business health\*\*.



The report is designed from a \*\*business and executive perspective\*\*, not just visual design.



---



**Dataset**

\- Retail sales dataset (Orders table)

\- Key fields used:

&nbsp; - Sales

&nbsp; - Profit

&nbsp; - Quantity

&nbsp; - Category

&nbsp; - Sub-Category

&nbsp; - Order Date



---



**Key Measures (DAX)**

The following measures were created to support dynamic analysis:



\- \*\*Total Sales\*\*

\- \*\*Total Profit\*\*

\- \*\*Profit Margin %\*\*

\- \*\*Total Orders\*\*



These measures respond dynamically to slicers and filters, enabling real-world analytical behavior.



---



**Report Pages**



 **Executive Overview**

\*\*Purpose:\*\* High-level business performance snapshot.



\*\*Includes:\*\*

\- Total Sales

\- Total Profit

\- Profit Margin %

\- Total Orders

\- Year slicer for time-based analysis



This page answers:

> \*“How is the business performing overall?”\*



---



&nbsp;**Profit \& Loss Insights**

\*\*Purpose:\*\* Identify profit drivers and loss-making areas.



\*\*Includes:\*\*

\- Profit by Sub-Category bar chart

\- Losses highlighted clearly

\- Category → Sub-Category slicer

\- Loss table filtered to show only negative profit items



This page answers:

> \*“Where are we making money, and where are we losing it?”\*



---



**Key Insights**

\- Most profit is driven by a small number of sub-categories

\- Certain sub-categories consistently generate losses

\- Loss identification helps prioritize cost control and strategy review

\- Measures enable flexible, slicer-driven analysis
